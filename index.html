About Carbon Footprints
Transport

A single long-haul flight emits more CO₂ per passenger than driving for an entire month. Cars burning petrol release ~2.3 kg CO₂ per litre of fuel.

Home Energy

Heating and electricity account for ~25% of household emissions. Switching to renewables or improving insulation can cut this by half.

Food & Diet

Beef production emits 60 kg CO₂ per kg of meat — 20× more than plant protein. Even two meat-free days a week saves ~0.5 t CO₂ per year.

Stuff & Shopping

Manufacturing a single smartphone emits ~70 kg CO₂. Fast fashion produces 10% of global emissions — more than aviation and shipping combined.

Global Context

Global CO₂ hit 37 Gt in 2024. To reach 1.5 °C safety, every person needs to reach 2 t/yr by 2050 — down from today's 4.7 t average.

Why It Matters

Each tonne of CO₂ warms the planet for centuries. Actions you take today ripple into the climate your children and grandchildren will inherit.

Common questions
How are my emissions calculated?
We use emission factors from the IPCC, Project Drawdown, and Our World in Data. Transport uses a standard 210 g CO₂/km for petrol cars; electricity uses an average grid intensity of 230 g/kWh. Results are estimates — your actual footprint varies by region, vehicle type, and lifestyle details.
Is my data saved or shared?
No. All calculations happen locally in your browser. We do not collect, store, or transmit any of your input data. Refreshing the page resets everything.
What is the 1.5 °C target?
The Paris Agreement aims to limit global warming to 1.5 °C above pre-industrial levels. To achieve this, average per-person emissions need to fall to roughly 2 tonnes CO₂ per year by 2050, down from the current world average of ~4.7 tonnes.
Can I embed or share EcoTrace?
Yes — EcoTrace is free to embed on any website or share as a standalone HTML file. Please credit Ayush Thakur as the creator. Commercial redistribution requires permission.
